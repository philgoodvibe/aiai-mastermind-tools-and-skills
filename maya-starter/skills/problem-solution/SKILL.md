# Problem-Solution

The most magnetic content names a problem the reader already feels, in words they already use. This lane mines how the ICP actually complains, then positions the agency as the calm answer. You are not inventing pain. You are quoting it back.

## Step 1: Read the brain

Read `brain/My_Agency_Context.md` (WHO I SERVE, MY COMMUNITY, MY STORY, the value proposition, agency name and contact) and `brain/voice/voice-profile.md`. Note which persona today's post targets. One post, one persona.

## Step 2: Mine complaints in their own words

Search where the ICP vents, and capture verbatim phrases, not summaries:

- Reddit (small business subs, local city subs, industry subs the ICP lives in)
- Google and Yelp reviews of insurance agencies in the area (low-star reviews are a goldmine for what people hate about the experience; never name the agency)
- Facebook groups, Nextdoor, and community forums for the ICP's trade or neighborhood
- The complaints can be about insurance, or about the ICP's wider life: their industry, employees, taxes, fees, paperwork, being too busy, feeling ignored

Collect 5 to 10 verbatim quotes with sources. The exact wording matters: "nobody picks up the phone" beats "poor customer service" every time.

## Step 3: Pick one problem

Choose the complaint that scores highest on: how often it appears, how emotionally raw the wording is, and how directly the agency's value proposition answers it. Save the unused quotes to `brain/what-worked.md` candidates for future posts.

## Step 4: Create the hook, visual concept, and copy

- **Hook:** use their words, or a sharpened version of them. "You need a COI by 8 AM and your agent's office is closed" outperforms "We offer responsive service."
- **Visual concept:** show the feeling of the problem moment (the unanswered phone, the 11 PM paperwork, the confused renewal letter). One image, one emotion. Pass this to image-brand.
- **Copy:** PAS is the default frame here. Problem (their words), Agitate (the real cost of living with it), Solution (how the agency handles it, with a story from `brain/voice/story-bank.md` if one fits).

## Step 5: Land the agency value proposition

The solution is the agency, stated the way the brain states it, in plain language. No jargon, no education-speak: explain what it means for them. Close with a soft, compliant CTA in the approved-phrases style: "Give us a call and we will help you review." Include agency name and contact in the caption and on the final visual.

## Compliance tripwires for this lane

This lane brushes against the rules more than any other. Before handoff, self-check:

- No named competitors or carriers, even when quoting a review about one
- No "you are overpaying" style absolutes; possibility language only
- No guarantees that the agency fixes the problem; show how it works instead
- Complaints are quoted anonymously, with usernames and identifying details removed

## Step 6: Hand off

1. Call the **image-brand** skill with the visual concept.
2. Run the **compliance-check** skill on the complete draft.
3. On PASS, create the post folder in `output/drafts/` per `output/README.md` and proceed to **posting-mmm**. On FAIL, revise per the exact concern and re-check.
