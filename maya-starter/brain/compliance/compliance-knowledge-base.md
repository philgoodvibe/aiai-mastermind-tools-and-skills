## 1. Core language rules (apply to every post)

### Rule: Soften absolute claims about other people's coverage or price

You do not know a stranger's policy, so never tell them what is true about it. State possibility, not fact.

| Never say | Say instead |
|---|---|
| "You are overpaying for insurance" | "You might be overpaying for insurance" |
| "Your policy has gaps" | "Many policies we review have gaps. Yours might too" |
| "You are with the wrong company" | "Many business owners we review are with a carrier that does not fit their risk, and that is often why the price is higher. That is how insurance pricing works" |

### Rule: No poaching language

Never tell someone to leave their current agent or carrier. Invite a conversation instead.

| Never say | Say instead |
|---|---|
| "Switch to us" | "Give us a call and we will help you review" |
| "Drop your agent" | "If you want a second set of eyes on your coverage, we are happy to look" |
| "We will beat their price" | "We will help you understand what you are paying for" |

### Rule: No guarantees, ever

No guaranteed savings, no guaranteed approval, no guaranteed claim outcomes, no "always," "never," "best," "cheapest," or "lowest." Insurance outcomes depend on underwriting and facts MAYA does not have.

| Never say | Say instead |
|---|---|
| "We guarantee you will save money" | "Many clients find savings or better-fitting coverage when we review" |
| "Your claim will be covered" | "We help you understand what your policy covers before you need it" |
| "The best rates in [CITY]" | "Coverage that fits your risk and your budget" |

### Rule: No pricing or savings claims

No specific premiums, percentages, or dollar amounts in marketing content. No "save 30 percent," no "policies from $99." If a real client story involves money, describe the outcome without numbers ("found coverage that fit her budget").

### Rule: No competitor or carrier disparagement

Never name a competitor or carrier negatively. Never mock another agent's work. The problem-solution lane quotes how clients feel about the industry, not attacks on named companies.

### Rule: Communicate in the client's language

Saying you serve a community lands harder in that community's own words. Do not announce it in English, demonstrate it.

| Weaker | Stronger |
|---|---|
| "We speak Vietnamese" | "Chúng tôi nói tiếng Việt" |
| "Se habla español" as a footnote | A full caption line in Spanish addressed to that reader |

Adapt this to the languages in brain/My_Agency_Context.md.

### Rule: Plain language, not insurance jargon

Clients do not want to be educated, they want things explained. Replace jargon with what it means for them. "Liability limits" becomes "how much the policy pays if you get sued."

### Rule: Stories must be real and anonymous

Client stories come from brain/voice/ with names and identifying details removed, unless written permission exists. Never fabricate a testimonial or a claim story.
