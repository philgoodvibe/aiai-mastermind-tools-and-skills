# Voice Profile Template

This is the structure MAYA produces when it writes `brain/voice/voice-profile.md`. It is here so you know what a finished voice profile contains, and so MAYA has a fixed shape to fill. MAYA builds the real file from the voice interview (see `voice-interview-prompt.md`), from transcripts of you talking, or from your My Voice document. Every section is grounded in your own words, never in generic assumptions.

The file is required. It is the gate MAYA checks every caption against before a draft is called done.

---

## How to read this template

Each section below names what MAYA fills in and why it matters. When MAYA writes the real `voice-profile.md`, it keeps these section headings and replaces the guidance with your specifics, quoting your actual phrasing wherever it can.

## 1. Speaking-style summary

Three to five sentences describing how you come across when you talk: warm and plainspoken, fast and funny, measured and reassuring, and so on. This is the headline a stranger would write after hearing you for two minutes.

## 2. Pace and rhythm

How your sentences move. Short and punchy, or long and winding. Where you pause. Whether you build to a point or lead with it. Whether you repeat for emphasis. Note the cadence, not just the speed.

## 3. Sentence patterns

The shapes you reach for. Do you ask rhetorical questions? Open with a statement and then qualify it? Use "here's the thing" or "look" to pivot? List the recurring structures so captions are built the way you actually build sentences.

## 4. Vocabulary

The words you use and the words you avoid. Your reading level when you talk to clients. Whether you use industry terms or translate them. Any words that would feel fake in your mouth. Note both the go-to words and the no-go words.

## 5. Signature phrases

The exact phrases that are unmistakably yours, captured verbatim. The way you greet, reassure, transition, and close. These are pulled straight from how you actually speak, not invented. If you did not say it, it does not go here.

## 6. Explanation style

How you make a hard thing simple. Do you use analogies, stories, numbers, step-by-step? Do you explain by comparison ("it's like..."), or by walking through what happens? This is how MAYA teaches a concept in a post and still sounds like you.

## 7. Emotional tone

The feeling underneath your words. Calm and steady, urgent and protective, playful, no-nonsense, big-hearted. How you handle frustration, fear, and good news. The default emotional register a reader should feel.

## 8. Audience relationship

How you treat the person you are talking to. Peer, trusted advisor, neighbor, protective expert. How much you assume they know. Whether you talk up, down, or across. This sets the posture of every caption.

## 9. Personality markers

The human details that make you you: humor style, what you get fired up about, your values, the things you would never say, recurring references to family, community, faith, sports, or your own story. The texture that separates you from every other agent.

## 10. Tuning Fork Sentence

One sentence, 15 to 30 words, written in your exact voice. If AI nails this one sentence, it nails everything: the rhythm, the vocabulary, the warmth, the posture. It is the reference note the whole voice is tuned against. MAYA writes it from your real phrasing and you confirm it sounds like you.

Example shape (yours will be in your own words):

> "Look, you don't need to understand every line of your policy. You need someone who reads it for you and picks up the phone when it matters. That's the whole job."

## 11. Voice Matching Checklist

The per-owner gate. A short checklist MAYA runs every caption against before calling it done. MAYA builds it from the sections above so it is specific to you, not generic. It typically asks:

- Does this sound like the Tuning Fork Sentence read aloud?
- Are the signature phrases present where they fit, and absent where they would feel forced?
- Is the vocabulary inside your range, with no words you would never say?
- Is the pace and rhythm yours, not AI-even?
- Is the emotional tone right for this post?
- Is the audience posture right (peer, advisor, neighbor, as defined above)?
- Would you read this out loud to a client without changing a word?

If any line fails, MAYA revises before the caption moves on. This checklist is the practical form of the whole profile, and MAYA updates it whenever you give voice feedback.
