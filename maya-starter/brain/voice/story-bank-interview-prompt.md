# Story Bank Interview Prompt (Optional)

The story bank is optional but highly recommended. Set up the system first and add stories later. If you do not have it, do not worry. Without it, MAYA still works from your voice profile and your agency context; it just will not write personal-story posts until you add it. You can come back and build this any time.

When you are ready, paste the block below into the Claude Code panel in VS Code. Plan for 30 to 45 minutes, or split it across sittings. MAYA interviews you for 20 to 30 real stories, removes the names and private details, tags each to a content pillar, and writes them into `brain/voice/story-bank.md`.

---

```
Interview me to build my story bank. Ask me one question at a time and dig
for the specifics: what happened, who was involved (no real names in the
final version), what went wrong, what it cost, what changed, and what I
learned. After each answer, give a short, warm follow-up that pulls out the
detail that makes the story land. I want 20 to 30 stories total. Cover these
areas:

- Why I got into this industry, and what almost made me quit
- The client situations I am proudest of
- Claims or losses that taught me something
- Times I told a client a hard truth
- My community: who I serve, in their words
- Mistakes I made early and what I do differently now
- Family and personal moments that shaped how I do business
- Wins that had nothing to do with insurance

After the interview, write each story into brain/voice/story-bank.md with:
a title, the story in 3 to 6 sentences in my voice, names and private
details removed, and a tag for which content pillar it serves
(Relate, Reveal, Guide, or Invite).
```

---

You can do this in more than one sitting. Tell MAYA "continue my story bank interview" and it picks up where you left off.

A note on the pillars, so the tags make sense:

- **Relate** stories make the reader feel seen. The everyday struggles, the funny moments, the "that's me too."
- **Reveal** stories show what you know that they do not. The disaster you saw coming, the gap nobody noticed.
- **Guide** stories teach. The hard truth you told, the mistake you help people avoid.
- **Invite** stories open a door. The moment a client reached out and it changed things for them.

Every story stays real and anonymous. Invented client stories are a compliance failure, so this interview is the only source MAYA uses for personal-story posts.
